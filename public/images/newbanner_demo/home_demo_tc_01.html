eHOMD provides comprehensive curated information on bacteria in the human mouth and aerodigestive tract, including the pharynx, nasal passages, sinuses and esophagus. There are 775 oral bacterial species. 57% are officially named, 13% unnamed but cultivated and 30% are known only as uncultivated phylotypes. eHOMD provides a provisional naming scheme for the currently unnamed taxa, based on the 16S rRNA sequence phylogeny, so that strain, clone and probe data from any laboratory can be directly linked to a stably named reference scheme.
